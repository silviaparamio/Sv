About Me

I am a Professor of Spanish at Saddleback College with over 12 years of experience in higher education. My teaching and research focus on Latin American literature, history, and culture, with particular expertise in colonial period texts and cultural preservation themes.

My pedagogical approach emphasizes student-centered learning, innovative educational technology integration, and culturally authentic engagement with Spanish language and literature. I am committed to moving beyond traditional grammar-focused instruction toward critical thinking-based approaches that honor the complexity of historical narratives and cultural experiences.

Teaching Philosophy

My courses emphasize "spiraling mastery" where concepts reappear in increasingly complex contexts, promoting deep understanding rather than superficial memorization. I balance academic rigor with accessibility, creating materials that challenge students while providing appropriate scaffolding for diverse learning needs.

Diversity, Equity & Inclusion Commitment

My commitment to DEI principles is fundamental to my teaching philosophy and practice. This commitment manifests in multiple dimensions of my work:

Curricular Representation

  • Centering indigenous and marginalized voices in Latin American literature courses
  • Examining colonial power structures and resistance strategies through diverse perspectives
  • Including texts like Visión de los vencidos that present indigenous perspectives on conquest
  • Challenging simplified historical narratives in favor of complex, nuanced understandings

Accessible Pedagogy

  • Developing OER materials to reduce financial barriers for students
  • Creating scaffolded learning experiences that support diverse language proficiency levels
  • Implementing student-centered approaches that honor varied learning styles and backgrounds
  • Using technology to create multiple pathways to learning and demonstration of knowledge

Critical Consciousness

  • Encouraging students to analyze how literacy and education have been used as tools of power
  • Fostering critical thinking about cultural narratives and historical interpretation
  • Creating classroom environments where students question assumptions and explore complexity
  • Emphasizing cultural authenticity and respect in language instruction
